Mediation analysis aims to explore the mechanism through which an independent variable influences a dependent variable.

For water temperature, if it directly affects the health or vitality of the fish, it could mediate the relationship between hopper type and survival time. If we observe the plot though, we do not see any pattern in the scatter.Vitality class of the fish might be a mediator if it reflects the health condition of the fish after being caught and sorted.   1. Is vitality class a good summary of the survival risk of the fish?

The vitality class appears to be a significant predictor of survival risk for the fish in the given analysis. The coefficients associated with vitality classes (Vitality_ClassB, Vitality_ClassC, and Vitality_ClassD) indicate a consistent decrease in log-odds in survival compared to the reference level. Specifically, the coefficients are as follows:

Vitality_ClassB: Approximately -0.793 log-odds

Vitality_ClassC: Approximately -1.641 log-odds

Vitality_ClassD: Approximately -1.018 log-odds

These negative coefficients suggest that as you move from the reference level (presumably another vitality class) to classes B, C, and D, there is a decreasing likelihood of survival. Therefore, lower vitality classes are associated with higher survival risks.
